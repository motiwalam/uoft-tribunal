Trial Division – s. B.i.1(b) and s. B.i.1(d) of Code – unauthorized aid or assistance  – plagiarism – final exam and an assignment – Student did not attend the hearing – Panel was satisfied the hearing could proceed in the Student’s absence – finding of guilt – Student used screenshots of answers to the exam to complete their exam – Student’s assignment was similar to that of another student – The University of Toronto v. D.S. (Case No. 451, August 24, 2007)  – in exceptional circumstances a penalty can be back dated but a principled reasons must be given for doing so; to do otherwise would result in a wide disparity amount sanctions – Panel found that since the Student did not participate in the hearing, it had no evidence on which it could find a principled reason to depart from the general rule – a grade of zero in the courses; three-year suspension; a four-year notation on the transcript; and a report to the Provost for publication.
