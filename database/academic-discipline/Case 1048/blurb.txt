Trial Division – s. B.i.1(a) of the Code – forged or falsified document – Student knowingly forged or in any other way altered or falsified a document required by the University, or the Student knowingly uttered, circulated or made use of that forged, altered or falsified document, namely a final exam booklet –  Student did not appear – Rules 9 and 17 of the University Tribunal’s Rules of Practice and Procedure (“Rules”) – ss. 6 and 7(3) of the Statutory Powers and Procedures Act (“SPPA”) – University’s Policy on Official Correspondence with Students – exam booklet contained identifying information that did not match the name of any of the students in the course – the Student signed in for the exam but an exam booklet for the Student was not submitted – expert evidence – a name, student ID, and student number originally written on the cover of the exam booklet was erased and replaced – finding of guilt – final grade of zero in the course; two-year suspension; three-year notation on transcript; and a report to the Provost for publication.
